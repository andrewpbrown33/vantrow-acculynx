# Runbook 05 — What to Ask a Lawyer (and When)

**Who:** Andrew + a trademark/commercial attorney. **When:** clearance starts as soon as
the name is chosen (Gate 1); the rest before public launch (Gate 2).

> **The automated screens in this repo are NOT legal clearance.** They are a cheap
> first-pass filter (`docs/legal/trademark-screens.md`). A registrable, defensible brand
> requires a professional clearance search and an attorney's judgment. Budget for this —
> renaming after launch costs far more.

## 1. Trademark clearance (start at Gate 1)

Bring: the chosen name, the top alternates, `docs/legal/trademark-screens.md`, and the
intended goods/services description ("cloud-based business-management software for
roofing and exterior contractors" — roughly Intl. Classes 9 and 42).

Ask for:
- [ ] Full clearance search (federal + state + common-law) on the chosen name.
- [ ] Opinion on similarity risk vs **AccuLynx** specifically (they are a well-funded
      incumbent in the same class) and vs the competitor names listed in
      `docs/research/acculynx/10-market-landscape.md`.
- [ ] Filing an intent-to-use (§1(b)) federal application if clearance passes.
- [ ] Whether to file the parent "Vantrow" mark too, if not already registered.

## 2. Comparative advertising review (before Gate 2)

Bring: the live `/vs-acculynx` draft and its claims table (claim → source → date), plus
`docs/legal/comparative-advertising-checklist.md`.

Ask for:
- [ ] Review of each comparative claim for substantiation and Lanham Act §43(a) risk.
- [ ] Sign-off on the nominative-use disclaimer wording.
- [ ] A view on bidding on "AccuLynx" as a paid-search keyword (do NOT do this before
      counsel weighs in).

## 3. Website terms + privacy (before Gate 2)

Bring: the `/privacy` and `/terms` drafts (marked TODO(legal) in the repo).

Ask for:
- [ ] Privacy policy covering the waitlist PII (name, email, company), Supabase as
      processor, and applicable state privacy laws (the form is open nationwide).
- [ ] Terms of use appropriate for a marketing site with a waitlist (no product yet).

## 4. Flag for later (not now, but tell counsel it's coming)

- [ ] Product terms of service + DPA when the platform launches (contractor + homeowner
      data, payments).
- [ ] Partner/integration agreements (measurement providers, suppliers, payment
      processors) — these have their own contract terms.
- [ ] Entity structure for the subsidiary ("<Brand>, a Vantrow company" — LLC vs d/b/a).

## You're done when

- Written (email is fine) clearance opinion on the name, and counsel has seen the
  comparison page and privacy/terms before they go live.
