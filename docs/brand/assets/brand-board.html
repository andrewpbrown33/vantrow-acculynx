Do / don't

  • Wordmark always lowercase, bold, "row" in camel; prose keeps "Eaverow".
  • Use accent-ink for camel-colored text on light surfaces; pure camel text only in the logotype or on the dark band.
  • Camel marks moments: milestones, paid states, bullets, eyebrows — one accent, used sparingly.
  • Keep the parent tie visible: "a Vantrow company" links to getvantrow.com.
  • Don't copy more than the one camel value from Vantrow — Eaverow is a sibling, not a clone.
  • Don't fill buttons with camel or set camel body text on light backgrounds.
  • Don't restyle or soften anything on /vs-acculynx — claims, dates, and the disclaimer are legal furniture.
  • Don't imitate any competitor's layout, colors, or icons (clean-room protocol §6).
  • Don't repaint the platform's pipeline-stage hues in brand colors — they're semantic.
