# Comparative Advertising Checklist

Apply to every public page, post, or ad that names or references AccuLynx (or any
competitor) — especially `apps/site` route `/vs-acculynx`. Derived from
`docs/legal/clean-room-protocol.md` §5 and §7.

## Per-claim checklist

For **each** comparative claim, verify:

- [ ] **Factual & verifiable** — the claim states a checkable fact, not an opinion dressed
      as fact ("AccuLynx requires X" must be documented; "customers describe AccuLynx as
      dated" must cite reviews).
- [ ] **Sourced & dated** — footnote links to a public source with an access date; the
      source appears in `docs/research/acculynx/00-sources-log.md`.
- [ ] **Current** — re-verify claims older than 90 days before re-publishing; competitors
      ship. Add "as of <date>" where staleness is plausible.
- [ ] **Pricing hedged** — AccuLynx pricing is quote-based and unpublished. Only "customers
      report…" phrasing with dated citations, or omit. Never state their price as fact.
- [ ] **AI claims honest** — AccuLynx ships AI features; never claim they "have no AI."
      Differentiate on architecture and workflow, not AI's mere presence.
- [ ] **No disparagement** — compare capabilities; never characterize the company, its
      people, or motives.

## Page-level checklist

- [ ] AccuLynx mark used **nominatively only**: plain text, no AccuLynx logo, no stylized
      rendering, no use in our headlines implying affiliation or endorsement.
- [ ] Disclaimer present: "AccuLynx is a trademark of its owner. <Brand> is not
      affiliated with or endorsed by AccuLynx."
- [ ] **No AccuLynx screenshots or UI reproductions** anywhere public.
- [ ] No integration promises without signed agreements — "designed to integrate with" /
      "on our roadmap" phrasing only (protocol §8).
- [ ] No competitor names in paid-search ad copy or display URLs (bidding on competitor
      keywords is a separate risk decision for counsel — flag before doing it).
- [ ] Superlatives ("simplest", "fastest") either substantiated or clearly framed as
      opinion/goal ("built to be the simplest…").
